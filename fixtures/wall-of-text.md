# Overview

The system was originally conceived as a modest internal utility, but it subsequently expanded through a long and largely undocumented series of incremental additions into a sprawling and deeply interconnected platform that nobody fully understands today. No single individual currently employed by the organization is able to hold a complete and accurate mental model of how the various subsystems actually interact with one another under real production conditions during a serious incident. This situation developed gradually over a period of several years, during which the team was perpetually preoccupied with the competing and frequently urgent demands of shipping new features, resolving customer-reported incidents, and satisfying leadership. The critical operational knowledge required to run the platform resides not in any centralized or maintained form of documentation, but rather in the fallible and frequently contradictory recollections of whichever long-tenured engineers happen to remain available on any particular day. The consequence is that onboarding a newly hired engineer, or safely modifying any component whose behavior is entangled with poorly understood assumptions embedded elsewhere, has become an exercise fraught with hidden risk and unpredictable delay for everyone involved. Any change, however small it may appear at the outset, can propagate in unforeseen ways through the loosely coupled and inadequately tested boundaries between modules that were never actually designed to be considered together as a coherent whole. Although numerous postmortems have been dutifully written in the aftermath of the recurring incidents that this pervasive fragility inevitably produces, these documents are almost never revisited and the corrective action items they carefully enumerate are almost never actually completed by anyone. The same underlying causes therefore continue to generate the same categories of failure quarter after quarter, while the ever-growing backlog of deferred reliability work steadily accumulates far beyond any realistic prospect of ever being meaningfully addressed. It is against this discouraging and self-perpetuating institutional backdrop that the present document, which makes absolutely no pretense of constituting a comprehensive or even a particularly coherent solution to any of it, has been somewhat reluctantly and belatedly composed by the author.
